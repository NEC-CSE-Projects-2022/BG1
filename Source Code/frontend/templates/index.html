Comparative Analysis of Quantum and Deep Learning Models

Benchmarking Quantum Neural Networks and Classical CNNs for Skin Lesion Classification

Research Motivation

Early and accurate classification of skin lesions is critical for preventing severe medical complications, including skin cancer. Traditional diagnosis relies heavily on expert interpretation, which can be subjective and time-consuming. This research explores whether emerging quantum machine learning models can compete with well-established deep learning architectures under identical conditions.

Models Evaluated

Quantum Models

  • Quantum Neural Network (QNN)
  • Quantum Support Vector Classifier (QSVC)

Classical Deep Learning

  • EfficientNet-B0
  • MobileNetV3
  • ResNet18
  • EFAM-Net

Key Findings

Experimental results show that classical deep learning models significantly outperform quantum-based approaches in skin lesion classification. EfficientNet-B0 and MobileNetV3 achieved over 90% validation accuracy, while quantum models were limited to 62–65% due to simulator and circuit depth constraints.

Dataset Used

The experiments were conducted on the publicly available HAM10000 dataset, consisting of dermoscopic images across multiple skin lesion categories. Stratified splitting and data augmentation were applied to address class imbalance.
